About Me

I am an Assistant Professor at the University of AlQadisiyah, specialising in artificial intelligence. My professional journey blends academic exploration and practical research, particularly in AI, machine learning, and speech technology. Over the years, I have contributed significantly to the field through research and applications in image processing, speaker verification, deep learning, and AI models.

Alongside my academic work, I also serve as Head of the Computer Center and Head of the Ibn Sina Division for E-learning at the University of AlQadisiyah. I have collaborated with researchers from Hungary, the United Kingdom, the United States, and Australia, and participated in the “Research England” project through Buckinghamshire New University (Grant Reference REX100-18).

I completed my Master’s in Computer Science at Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Marathwada University, focusing on image processing. My experience shaped my approach to applying emerging technologies in satellite image processing and AI.

My research interests include image processing, medical image analysis, speaker verification, and forensic voice comparison, bridging theory with real-world applications through hands-on projects and experiments.
